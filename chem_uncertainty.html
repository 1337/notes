Heisenburg Uncertainty Principle

The Heisenburg uncertainty principle states that $\Delta x \Delta p_x \geq h$ for a particle in a 1D box.

$\begin{eqnarray*} p_x &=& \sqrt{2mE} \\ &=& \sqrt{2m(\frac{n^2 h^2}{8mL^2})} \\ &=& \pm \frac{nh}{2L} \\ \Delta p_x &=& 2 \frac{nh}{2L} \\ &=& \frac{nh}{L}\\ \end{eqnarray*}$

You'll notice that the energy equation above had an extra $L$. I have no idea why.

Because the box is exactly $L$ wide, the max error for $x$ is $L$.

$\Delta x = L$

This gives you:

$\begin{eqnarray*} \Delta x \Delta p_x &=& L \cdot \frac{nh}{L} \\ &=& nh \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

(the minimum error is therefore just $h$.)
