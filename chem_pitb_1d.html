Particle in a Box: Time-independent, 1D version

The schrodinger equation remains $\hat{H} \Psi = E \Psi$. However, we have two boundary conditions:

$\Psi(0) = 0,\ \Psi(L) = 0$

So now we solve the equation

$\begin{eqnarray*} \hat{H} \Psi &=& E \Psi \\ \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 \Psi (x) &=& E \Psi (x) \\ \frac{\hbar^2}{2m} \nabla^2 \Psi (x) - E \Psi (x) &=& 0 \\ \nabla^2 \Psi (x) + \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} \Psi (x) &=& 0 \\ \frac{\partial^2}{\partial x^2} \Psi (x) + \frac{2mE}{\hbar^2} \Psi (x) &=& 0 \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Solve the differential equation, and obtain roots: $\Psi(x) = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} = \pm \frac{i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}$

And so the general solution is:

$\Psi(x) = A e^{\frac{+i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} + B e^{\frac{-i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x}$

Using the first boundary condition ($\Psi(0) = 0$),

$\begin{eqnarray*} \Psi(x) &=& A e^{\frac{+i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} + B e^{\frac{-i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} \\ 0 &=& A e^{\frac{+i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}(0)} + B e^{\frac{-i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}(0)} \\ 0 &=& A + B \\ A &=& -B \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Substituting $B = -A$ back into the above to get

$\begin{eqnarray*} \Psi(x) &=& A e^{\frac{+i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} + (-A) e^{\frac{-i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} \\ \Psi(x) &=& A (e^{\frac{+i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} - e^{\frac{-i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x}) \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Expand the equation using Euler's Equation. You should get

$\begin{eqnarray*} \Psi(x) &=& A (e^{\frac{+i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x} - e^{\frac{-i}{\hbar}\sqrt{2mE}x}) \\ \Psi(x) &=& A (\cos{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}x} + i\sin{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}x} - \cos{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}x} + i\sin{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}x}) \\ \Psi(x) &=& A (2i\sin{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}x}) \\ \Psi(x) &=& C (\sin{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}x}) \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Then notice that $\Psi(L) = 0$, so either $C$ is 0 or $\sin{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}(L)}$ is 0.
$C$ won't be 0 because that would be stupid ("the particle does not exist").
$\sin{\frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}(L)}$ is 0 when the contents are a multiple of $\pi$:

$\begin{eqnarray*} \frac{\sqrt{2mE}}{\hbar}(L) &=& n \pi \\ \sqrt{2mE} &=& \frac{n \pi\hbar}{L} \\ 2mE &=& (\frac{n \pi\hbar}{L})^2 \\ E &=& \frac{1}{2m} (\frac{n \pi\hbar}{L})^2 \\ E &=& \frac{n^2 h^2}{8mL} \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

And therefore:

$\Psi(x) = C (\sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x})$.

Solving for $C$

C is still unknown. Because we know the particle has 100% probability of being in the box, we can use that fact to find $C$.

$\begin{eqnarray*} 1 &=& \int^{\infty}_{-\infty} \Psi^{*} \Psi d\tau \\ &=& \int^{\infty}_{-\infty} C^{*}(\sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x}) C (\sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x}) dx \\ &=& C^{*}C \int^{\infty}_{-\infty} (\sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x}) (\sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x}) dx \\ &=& |C|^2 \int^{L}_{0} \sin^2{\frac{n \pi}{L}x} dx \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Resolve $sin^2$ using the double angle formula:

$\begin{eqnarray*} 1 &=& |C|^2 \int^{L}_{0} \frac{1}{2} - \cos{\frac{2 n \pi}{L}x} dx \\ 1 &=& |C|^2 [\frac{1}{2}x - \frac{1}{2}\sin{\frac{2 n \pi}{L}x}]^{L}_{0} \\ 1 &=& |C|^2 [\frac{1}{2}(L) - \frac{1}{2}\sin{\frac{2 n \pi}{L}(L)} - \frac{1}{2}(0) + \frac{1}{2}\sin{\frac{2 n \pi}{L}(0)}] \\ 1 &=& |C|^2 [\frac{1}{2}(L) - (0) - (0) + (0)] \\ 1 &=& |C|^2 \cdot \frac{L}{2} \\ \frac{2}{L} &=& |C|^2 \\ C &=& \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}}. \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Put that back into $\Psi(x) = C (\sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x})$, and you're done!

$\Psi(x) = \sqrt{\frac{2}{L}} \sin{\frac{n \pi}{L}x}$ (for inside the box)

Implications

We got the result above that $E = \frac{n^2 h^2}{8mL}$. So, by quantum number:

$n$ $E_n$ Nodes
1 $\frac{h^2}{8mL}$ $n-1$
2 $\frac{4 h^2}{8mL}$ $n-1$
3 $\frac{9 h^2}{8mL}$ $n-1$
4 $\frac{16 h^2}{8mL}$ $n-1$
