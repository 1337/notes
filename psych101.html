Pavlov and his dogs Pavlov Behaviourism (classical conditioning)
  • Pavlov notices that spit is secreted before the food goes to the dog ...
  • Normally if a metronome goes up to a dog, the dog doesn't spit
  • Normally if food (the unconditioned stimulus: what is native) goes up to a dog, the dog spits
  • If food and metronome go up to a dog, the dog spits
  • Now if the metronome goes up to a dog, the dog spits
  • The metronome is the conditioned (neutral) stimulus: what is learnt
Learning and unlearning:
  • In classical conditioning, learning is the association of a conditioned stimulus or neutral stimulus with an unconditioned stimulus to the extent that the conditioned stimulus produces the unconditioned response
  • Discrimination: reverse of generalization
  • learn to be scared of one thing but not another
  • Extinction: conditioned stimulus no longer produces the response
  • Occurs if the unconditioned stimulus is no longer paired with the conditioned stimuli
  • Problems with Classical Conditioning
  • can't teach them new tricks (dogs spit, albert gets afraid)

  • The metronome is the conditioned (neutral) stimulus
  • Learning and unlearning
  • In classical conditioning, learning is the association of a conditioned stimulus or neutral stimulus with an unconditioned stimulus to the extent that the conditioned stimulus produces the unconditioned response

  • Problems with Classical Conditioning
