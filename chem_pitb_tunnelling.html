Particle in a Box: Tunnelling

If a potential wall is not infinity, a particle can be outside the well even though it doesn't have enough energy to go over the wall.

We set up three regions' worth of schrodinger equations:

$\begin{eqnarray*} \Psi_{I}(x) &=& A e^{i \alpha x} + B e^{-i \alpha x} \\ \Psi_{II}(x) &=& C e^{\beta x} + D e^{\beta x} \\ \Psi_{III}(x) &=& F e^{i \alpha x} \end{eqnarray*}$

$\Psi_{III}$ has only one term because it is a travelling wave in only one direction.
$\Psi_{II}$ doesn't have imaginary parts because it is a decaying function, not a standing wave.

Transmission Coefficient

The transmission coefficient is the percentage of the wavefunction leaking across the wall... so it is

$\frac{1}{\tau} = \frac{|A|^2}{|F|^2}$
$\tau ~= 4e^{\frac{-a}{\hbar}\sqrt{2m(V_o - E}}$

You can see that it depends on the wall's height, $V_0$.
