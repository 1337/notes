Operators and Commutators

Operators are things with a little hat on top (e.g. $\hat{H}$). They operate on your variable.

Example

If $\hat{A} = [\frac{d}{dx} + x]$, then

$\begin{eqnarray*} \hat{A}f &=& [\frac{d}{dx} + x]f \\ &=& \frac{d}{dx}f + xf \\ &=& f' + xf \\ \end{eqnarray*}$

Commutators are groups of two operators. If they have zero difference when their order is swapped, then we say the two operators commute.

$ [\hat{A}, \hat{B}]f = \hat{A}\hat{B}f - \hat{B}\hat{A}f $
