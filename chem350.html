W, the statistical weight, equals to the number of distinct microstates.

\begin{array}{rcl} W & = & \frac{N!}{\prod_{all\ i}n_{i}!}=\frac{N!}{\prod_{all\ i}a_{i}!} \\ \frac{\delta\ln{W}}{\delta a_i} & = & \frac{\delta}{\delta a_i}\ln(\frac{N!}{\prod_{all\ i}a_{i}!}) \\ & = & \frac{\delta}{\delta a_{i}}(\ln{N!}-\ln(\prod_{all\ i}a_{i}!)) \\ & = & -\frac{\delta \ln{a_{i}!}}{\delta a_{i}} \\ & = & -\ln{a_i} \end{array}

Equilibrium constants - chemical potential and molar Gibbs energy

Let N = N_A.

Let \bar{G}_\alpha be the chemical postential.

\begin{array}{rcl} \mu_\alpha & = & -N_A k_B T \ln{\frac{q_\alpha e}{N_A}} + PV \\ & = & -N_A k_B T \ln{\frac{q_\alpha}{N_A}} -N_A k_B T \ln{e} + N_A k_B T \\ & = & -N_A k_B T \ln{\frac{q_\alpha}{N_A}} \\ \end{array}

Now set a reference pressure P^0

\begin{array}{rcl} \frac{q_{\alpha}}{N_A} & = & q_{trans}^{\alpha} \times q_{int}^{\alpha} \\ & = & [\frac{V}{N_A\Lambda_{\alpha}^3}]\times q_{int}^{\alpha} \times [\frac{P^0}{P^0}] \\ & = & (\frac{1}{N_A\Lambda_{\alpha}^3}\frac{RT}{N_AP^0})(\frac{P_0}{P_{\alpha}}) \\ & = & \frac{q_{\alpha}^0}{N_A}\frac{P_0}{P_{\alpha}} \end{array}

where q_{\alpha}^0 is the q for species \alpha under standard pressure.

Molar Gibbs Energy

For n_bB + n_cC \rightleftharpoons n_dD + n_eE

\mu_x = -RT\ln{\frac{q^x}{N_A}}
\begin{array}{rcl} \Delta G & = & n_d\mu_d + n_e\mu_e - n_b\mu_b - n_c\mu_c \\ & = & -RT[n_d\ln{(...)} + n_e\ln{(...)} - n_b\ln{(...)} - n_c\ln{(...)}] \\ & = & -RT\ln{\bigl(\frac{ {(\frac{q_d}{N_A})}^{n_d}{(\frac{q_e}{N_A})}^{n_e}} { {(\frac{q_b}{N_A})}^{n_b}{(\frac{q_c}{N_A})}^{n_c}}\bigr)} \\ or & = & -RT\ln{(\frac{\prod_{products\ i}(\frac{q_i}{N_A})^{n_i}} {\prod_{reactants\ j}(\frac{q_j}{N_A})^{n_j}})} \end{array}

Notice that at equilibrium, \Delta G = -RT \ln{(1)} = 0, so \frac{\prod_{products\ i}(\frac{q_i}{N_A})^{n_i}} {\prod_{reactants\ j}(\frac{q_j}{N_A})^{n_j}} must be 1 at equilibrium.

Convert that using the conversion factor

\frac{q_\alpha}{N_A} = \frac{q_\alpha^0}{N_A}\frac{P_0}{P_\alpha}
\prod_{products} (\frac{q_i}{N_A})^{n_i} \rightarrow \prod_{products} (\frac{q_i^0}{N_A})^{n_i} (\frac{P_0}{P_i})^{n_i}

By isolating these pressure terms and picking P^0 = 1 bar/atm, we get (for the case of n_bB + n_cC \rightleftharpoons n_dD + n_eE)

\frac{(\frac{P_0}{P_d})^{n_d}(\frac{P_0}{P_e})^{n_e}} {(\frac{P_0}{P_b})^{n_b}(\frac{P_0}{P_c})^{n_c}} = \frac{(P_d)^{n_d}(P_e)^{n_e}} {(P_b)^{n_b}(P_c)^{n_c}} = K_p

Rate Laws

Zeroth order rate law:

Graph of [A] vs t is linear.

Rate = -\frac{d[A]}{dt}=k[A]^0=k\ (constant)

or

\frac{d[A]}{dt}=-k

Integration gives

[A]_t=-kt+C

At t=0,

[A]_0=-k(0)+C=C \rightarrow C=[A]_0

so

[A]_t=[A]_0-kt

First order rate law:

Graph of ln[A] vs t is linear.

Rate = -\frac{d[A]}{dt}=k[A]^1=k[A] \ (see\ how\ it\ depends\ on\ A?)

or

\frac{d[A]}{dt}=-k[A]

To integrate, you need to rearrange.

\frac{dy}{dx}=-ky\rightarrow\frac{dy}{y}=-kdx
\int{\frac{dy}{y}}=\ln{y}=-kt+C\rightarrow\ln{[A]_t}=-kt+C

At t=0,

[A]_0=-k(0)+C=C

so

ln{[A]_t}=ln{[A]_0}-kt

or

ln{[A]_t}-ln{[A]_0}=ln{\frac{[A]_t}{[A]_0}}=-kt

OR

[A]_t=[A]_0 e^{-kt}
Important

Only in first order rate laws are half lives constant: t_{\frac{1}{2}}=\frac{\ln{2}}{k}

Second order rate law:

Graph of 1/[A] vs t is linear.

Rate = -\frac{d[A]}{dt}=k[A]^1=k[A]^2

or

\frac{d[A]}{dt}=-k[A]^2

To integrate, you need to rearrange.

\frac{dy}{dx}=-ky^2\rightarrow\frac{dy}{y^2}=-kdx
\int{\frac{dy}{y^2}}=\int{-kdx}
\frac{1}{y}=-kt+C\rightarrow\frac{1}{[A]_t}=-kt+C

At t=0,

\frac{1}{[A]_0}=-k_0+C\rightarrow C=-\frac{1}{[A]_0}

so

\frac{1}{[A]_t}-\frac{1}{[A]_0}=kt

Order n rate law:

\frac{1}{ {[A]_t}^{n-1}}-\frac{1}{ {[A]_0}^{n-1}}=(n-1)kt
